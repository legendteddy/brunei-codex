Write one paragraph describing your target outcome, your non-negotiables, and the deadline. Add constraints such as budget cap, timeline limit, or compliance requirements. Clarity at this stage avoids expensive rework later [Source: Official portals listed in Source Notes].

### 2. Map requirements from official channels

Collect only current, source-backed requirements. If two pages conflict, treat the page with direct authority over the process as primary. Save the exact URL and the date you checked it.

### 3. Build a complete action pack

Create a folder with your IDs, forms, screenshots, correspondence, and comparison notes. A complete pack increases speed and reduces mistakes during submission or purchase.

### 4. Execute in sequence and log each checkpoint

Move one checkpoint at a time. Record submission dates, reference numbers, and follow-up dates. Sequence discipline is one of the strongest reliability controls in practical workflows.

### 5. Re-verify before final commitment

Before payment, submission, or travel, re-check any time-sensitive claim. Use this short rule: if it affects money, deadlines, or legal status, verify again.
