# Hari Raya Guide in Brunei - Complete Guide 2026

**Quick Answer:** In Brunei, Hari Raya planning works best when you separate date confirmation from execution. Use official public-holiday references for preliminary planning, but finalize schedules only after moon-sighting dependent outcomes are confirmed [Source: MOE Calendar 2026 PDF]. For households, the highest-impact controls are visit sequencing, food planning, and transport timing.

## Overview

Hari Raya can involve family visits, open-house planning, travel coordination, and budget pressure in the same week. Most problems happen when everything is decided too late. A practical system is:

1. Confirm date status from official channels.
2. Split hosting, visiting, and travel plans into separate checklists.
3. Set one coordinator for final confirmations.

What this looks like: instead of one long list, create three lists: `people`, `places`, and `payments`. This makes changes easier when dates or availability shift.

## Step-by-Step Process

### 1. Date and leave confirmation

Use published public-holiday references as planning anchors, then confirm final Islamic-date outcomes near the observance window [Source: MOE Calendar 2026 PDF].

For teams and families:

- Keep leave requests as provisional until final update.
- Communicate one final confirmation message once official status is clear.

### 2. Visit schedule design

Plan visits by geography, not by arrival order. A simple method:

1. Group households by district.
2. Assign day blocks.
3. Add travel buffers.
4. Confirm attendance windows.

This reduces wasted travel time and keeps visits manageable.

### 3. Hosting setup and food flow

Use two shopping rounds:

- Round A (3-5 days before): non-perishables and core hosting items.
- Round B (1-2 days before): perishables and final top-ups.

Editorial guidance: assign one person to inventory and one person to guest-flow timing. This avoids over-buying and kitchen bottlenecks.

### 4. Budget control for Raya week

A lot of overspending comes from many small, untracked purchases. Use a daily cap and write every spend category quickly on your phone:

- Food
- Gifts
- Fuel/transport
- Household supplies

## Practical Scenarios

### Scenario A: New expat family with limited local network

If you are newly arrived and have a smaller network, keep your first Hari Raya simple:

- Accept invitations you can confirm clearly.
- Keep one low-complexity home hosting window.
- Prioritize travel efficiency over ambitious social volume.

This gives you space to learn local rhythm without overcommitting.

### Scenario B: Multi-household visit day

For days with many visits:

1. Start with the farthest location first.
2. Group nearby homes into one block.
3. Keep fixed meal and prayer break windows.
4. Communicate delay updates early.

This reduces fatigue and keeps the day respectful for hosts and guests.

## Risk Controls Checklist

Before Raya week, run this checklist:

- Date status confirmed from official channel references.
- Hosting shopping split into two stages.
- Budget cap written and shared in household plan.
- Driver/transport responsibilities assigned.
- One shared itinerary link distributed to key family members.

Even a short checklist can reduce most last-minute stress points.
